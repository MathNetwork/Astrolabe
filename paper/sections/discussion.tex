\subsection{Papers Are Not the Primary Form of Knowledge}
\label{sec:disc-papers}

% - Academic papers exist to satisfy three needs: knowledge management (organization, retrieval), attribution (credit, priority), and carrier (dissemination, archival). These needs are real, but papers as the tool satisfying them are a product of historical constraints, not the primary form of knowledge itself
% - A LaTeX paper is a linear document — it flattens inherently networked knowledge (definitions depend on definitions, theorems cite lemmas) into a one-dimensional sequence. Chapter ordering is the author's narrative choice, not knowledge's intrinsic structure
% - The categorical obj/mor model directly expresses knowledge's intrinsic structure: objects are knowledge units, morphisms are their relationships, sorts are type labels. This structure doesn't depend on any particular linearization (paper) or rendering format (LaTeX/PDF)
% - Decentralized knowledge management: when every obj and mor has a content-addressed hash, knowledge unit identity doesn't depend on which paper, database, or institution it resides in. Different Astrolabe instances can merge knowledge networks via hash matching — true decentralization
% - Consistent with author's prior work (Toward Decentralized Formal Mathematics): content-addressing is the foundation for decentralization, categorical data model is the foundation for structuring, combining both achieves decentralized structured knowledge management

\begin{abstract}
We define the Astrolabe category, a free category generated by a signature $(O, M, h)$ in which objects and morphisms carry open information records and share a global identifier space. We prove that this category is well-defined, that quotient categories induced by morphisms are well-defined, and that an Astrolabe universe satisfying an embedding axiom admits enrichment and quotient closure. We establish that any source-target-preserving signature morphism extends to an import functor, and that layered rendering functors compose via well-defined natural transformations. All results are illustrated with concrete instances from a reference implementation.
\end{abstract}

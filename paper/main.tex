\begin{abstract}
Every piece of structured knowledge is a finite string over a finite alphabet. We define the \emph{Astrolabe}: a semantic simplex $\sigma \in H^+ \times H \times R$ whose reference field is unconstrained. Restriction maps satisfy $\partial^2 = 0$, yielding a chain complex on which homological algebra---homology, long exact sequences, the snake lemma---operates unconditionally. Adding the classical constraint (all references point to vertices) recovers the geometric interpretations of algebraic topology. Adding LLM embeddings gives exterior algebra geometry; topology and geometry coexist, and their inconsistency pinpoints structural gaps. We prove an \emph{embedding obstruction theorem}: the topological complexity of a knowledge base gives a lower bound on embedding dimension, and give formal characterizations of hallucination, scaling saturation, ``understanding vs.\ memorization,'' and chain-of-thought reasoning. Structure-preserving maps between Astrolebes form the category $\mathbf{Ast}$, whose universal properties characterize the plugin architecture. The algebraic core is mechanically verified in Lean~4 (zero \texttt{sorry}). The data format \texttt{astrolabe.json} and a reference implementation are open-source.
\end{abstract}

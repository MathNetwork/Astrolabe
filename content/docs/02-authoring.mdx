# Writing with Cards

A project's documents are not where the mathematics lives — the [data model](/docs/data-model)
stores every statement as a content-addressed **card**. Documents are `.mdx` files
(in the project's `.astrolabe/docs/`) that *compose* those cards into readable
chapters: ordinary prose and math, with two macros that pull cards in.

## `\entryblock` — place a card

`\entryblock{hash}` renders the full card at that spot: its sort-colored frame,
derived number, title, and statement body. Clicking a card selects it in the
network view. Three forms:

- `\entryblock{hash}` — the card, as a block.
- `\entryblock{hash}{collapsible}` — collapsed by default; the reader expands it.
- `\entryblock{hash}{ …nested content… }` — a card with content inside its body,
  including other `\entryblock`s. Nesting is purely visual — it does not create
  any relation between the cards, and a nested card is numbered exactly as if it
  stood alone.

Where a card's `\entryblock` first appears is what gives it its number (below).

## `\entryref` — cite a card inline

`\entryref` is a link, not a placement — it never affects numbering:

- `\entryref{hash}` — automatic display: the card's sort and number
  ("Theorem 7.2.8"), or its citation key for a bibliography card ("[MT07]").
- `\entryref{hash}{text}` — your own link text.

Either way the reference resolves to the card's *home* number — where its
`\entryblock` first appears in the project — so a citation in chapter 3 to a
card placed in chapter 7 reads "7.2.8" everywhere.

## How numbers are assigned

Numbers are **derived, never stored**: a card gets a positional coordinate
`chapter.section.item` from where its `\entryblock` first appears in the
project's documents — proofs and `\entryref`s never count. The full rules have
their own page: [derived numbering](/docs/numbering).

## Everything else is Markdown

Documents are standard MDX: GitHub-flavored Markdown, `$…$` and `$$…$$` KaTeX
math (macros included), headings, lists, tables. One more macro is available:
`\status` renders a live formalization-status summary of the project's store.
